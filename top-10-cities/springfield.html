History of Springfield

Springfield, Illinois, steeped in rich American history, serves as the state capital and has played a pivotal role since its founding in the early 19th century. The city is indelibly linked to Abraham Lincoln, who lived here from 1837 until his presidency in 1861, and it's home to a wealth of historical sites connected to his legacy.

Read more about Springfield's History

Springfield Culture

Cultural life in Springfield is vibrant, with an emphasis on history and the arts.The Abraham Lincoln Presidential Library and Museum, along with numerous historic sites, theaters, and art galleries, enriches the cultural fabric of the city, reflecting a deep appreciation for the past and present.

Read more about Springfield's Culture

The Springfield Economy

Springfield's economy is anchored by the state government, healthcare, education, and tourism, with the presence of the state government being a major economic driver. These sectors contribute to a stable economic landscape, providing numerous opportunities for residents and businesses alike.
