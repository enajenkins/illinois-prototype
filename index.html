History of Illinois

Illinois has a rich pre-European history, with American Indian cultures inhabiting the region for thousands of years. The Cahokia complex, representing the Mississippian culture, was a significant urban center featuring mounds, plazas, and ceremonial structures.

Read more about Illinois's History

Illinois Culture

Springfield, Illinois, steeped in rich American history, serves as the state capital and has played a pivotal role since its founding in the early 19th century. The city is indelibly linked to Abraham Lincoln, who lived here from 1837 until his presidency in 1861, and it's home to a wealth of historical sites connected to his legacy.

Obscure Facts About Illinois

  1. Abraham Lincoln's Home: Explore the only home Abraham Lincoln ever owned, now a National Historic Site. Learn about the Lincoln family's life
  2. Cozy Dog Drive-In: Visit the birthplace of the corn dog, Cozy Dog Drive-In, which claims to have invented this popular fair food. Discover more about its history
  3. Lincoln's Tomb: Pay your respects at the final resting place of Abraham Lincoln and his family. Plan your visit to the Lincoln Tomb
  4. Shea's Gas Station Museum: Immerse yourself in the nostalgia of a 1920s gas station at Shea's Gas Station Museum. Learn about the vintage gas pumps
  5. Route 66 Drive-In: Experience a blast from the past at the Route 66 Drive-In, one of the few remaining drive-in theaters along the historic Route 66. Check out the current showings
  6. The Dana-Thomas House: Explore the stunning architecture of the Dana-Thomas House, designed by Frank Lloyd Wright. Plan your visit to this Prairie School
  7. Illinois State Fair: Attend the Illinois State Fair, an annual event featuring agriculture, entertainment, and carnival rides. Check out the fair's attractions
  8. Horseshoes: Discover the culinary tradition of horseshoes in Springfield, a dish featuring toast, meat, and fries covered in cheese sauce. Find a local spot to try this unique meal
  9. Illinois State Museum: Visit the Illinois State Museum to explore exhibits on natural history, anthropology, and art. Plan your visit to the museum
  10. Old State Capitol: Step back in time at the Old State Capitol, where Abraham Lincoln served as a legislator. Learn about the history of this historic site
